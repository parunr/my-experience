How I work

Five patterns, sixteen years.

The operating signatures that show up in every era of the work.

  1. Finding the undocumented failure mode

    From CMMI audits at IBM to the MCP publisher-prefix mismatch at Avanade — I don't accept "it should work" as a diagnostic conclusion. The signal is almost always three layers below where the failure surfaces.

  2. Building and winning simultaneously

    Delivery leadership and business development aren't separate roles. I delivered a major FMCG client's transition while winning two EMEA retail/wholesale RFPs, ran the 60-person B2B2C programme while building Accenture's APO capability, and led the $2M retail operations team while closing multi-million-dollar AO/SI deals.

  3. Building observability before declaring victory

    From CMMI at IBM through Application Insights CI/CD at Accenture/Avanade to OpenTelemetry with PII masking on local agents — I build the infrastructure to see inside systems before I call them done. You can't fix what you can't see; you can't trust what you can't measure.

  4. Translating ambiguity into reusable architecture

    The generic SAP ERP template at Infosys. The Integrated Solutioning Workbench at Accenture. Repeatable AI patterns and the Weather Copilot reference architecture at Avanade. In every era, the pattern is the same: solve the immediate problem, then abstract the solution into something others can build on.

  5. Operating across the full stack

    Electronics & Communications engineering, SAP ERP delivery, cloud enterprise architecture, corporate strategy, agentic AI, executive stakeholder management. Full-stack mobility means I identify where a failure actually originates — not just where it surfaces — and design solutions at the right layer.
